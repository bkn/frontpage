The Bibliographic Knowledge Network (BKN or BibKN) is a loose coalition of partners funded partly by the NSF Cyber-enabled Discovery and Innovation (CDI) Program. The mission is to build free tools and applications for researchers to distill, normalize, reconcile, publish, and analyze large open bibliographic datasets, and to develop a network of websites and webservices participating in this activity.

The major participating organizations are the American Institute of Mathematics (AIM), Harvard University, Stanford University and the University of Californa at Berkeley.

Research supported in part by NSF Award 0835851, Bibliographic Knowledge Network.
